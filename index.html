Daily Plan

Detailed Itinerary

July 1-2

Seattle -> Paris -> Geneva

The trip begins with an overnight flight from Seattle to Geneva, connecting through Paris and arriving late morning on July 2.

  • Outbound flight: Seattle 1:30 PM on DL8763, connect in Paris, then DL8243 to Geneva.
  • Arrive Geneva at 11:10 AM on July 2.
  • From Geneva airport, bus 23 goes toward Avenue de l'Ain, then a short walk to the Chatelaine address.
  1. Jul 1 13:30
    Depart Seattle

    Leave SEA on Delta DL8763, operated by Air France, bound for Paris CDG.

  2. Jul 2 10:00
    Paris to Geneva

    Connect to DL8243 for Geneva. Keep essentials and a clean shirt in the carry-on.

  3. Jul 2 11:10
    Arrive Geneva

    Use the first day for recovery, transit, and light walking rather than heavy sightseeing.

July 4-5

Chamonix, Les Houches, and TMB start

Arrive in Chamonix, meet the group, settle into Les Houches, then start the TMB group tour the next morning.

  • July 4 hotel: Chalet Hotel du Bois, Les Houches.
  • Group context begins at 234 Avenue de Courmayeur, Chamonix-Mont-Blanc.
  • Bus line 1 is noted for the Les Houches hotel transfer.
  1. Jul 4
    Arrive in Chamonix

    Meet the group and continue to Les Houches for the pre-trek hotel night.

  2. Jul 4 late PM
    Prepare trek bag

    Separate what goes with luggage transfer from what stays in the day pack.

  3. Jul 5 09:00
    TMB group meeting

    Meet at the parking lot in front of the Prarion cable car in Les Houches.

July 5-11

7-day Tour du Mont Blanc

The center of the trip is the guided TMB group trek. The appendix notes a 12-person group, shared rooms, meals, guide, luggage transfer, and selected transport.

  • Group size noted as 12 people per group.
  • Breakfast, lunch, and dinner are included during the tour.
  • Tour cost noted as 1455 euros per person.
  • Finish: July 11 around 4:30 PM in Le Tour, France. Local train ticket to Chamonix provided.
  1. Day 1
    Start from Les Houches

    The first day includes a cable car ticket. First-day hotel-to-trailhead transport may require extra payment if needed.

  2. Days 2-6
    Guided alpine hiking

    Follow the guide's pace, weather calls, and daily luggage transfer instructions.

  3. Day 7
    Finish in Le Tour

    Return toward Chamonix and Les Houches, then retrieve stored luggage from the July 4 hotel.

July 12

Extra Chamonix day

A recovery and regrouping day after the trek. The plan notes morning luggage drop at Chamonix Lodge and coordination with Yang and Lv on departure timing.

  • Hotel: Chamonix Lodge for July 12-13.
  • Take Chamonix city bus line 1 from Les Houches - Les Esserts toward Chamonix.
  • Transit note: Uber around 10 minutes, bus around 30 minutes.
  1. Morning
    Move to Chamonix

    Check in or leave luggage at Chamonix Lodge before starting the day.

  2. Midday
    Open Chamonix time

    Keep this day flexible after seven hiking days. Good slot for laundry, supplies, and a slow meal.

  3. Evening
    Prepare for Zermatt

    Confirm train timing and make sure everyone has the Switzerland transit plan ready.

July 13

Chamonix -> Zermatt, then Riffelsee if weather allows

The Zermatt strategy is to use the first good weather window immediately: town walk first, then Rotenboden, Riffelsee, and Gornergrat in late afternoon light.

  • Amy hotel: Hotel Garni Testa Grigia, Zermatt. Group lodging noted as VRBO on Wiestistrasse / Zer Baennu.
  • Classic photo target: Riffelsee Matterhorn reflection.
  • Ride the Gornergrat Railway toward Rotenboden and Gornergrat.
  1. 14:00
    Arrive Zermatt and check in

    Take a quick town walk: Hinterdorf, Bahnhofstrasse, and the church square.

  2. 16:00
    Go up toward Rotenboden

    Walk 5-10 minutes to Riffelsee for the classic Matterhorn reflection angle.

  3. 19:30
    Train back down

    Return to Zermatt around 8:15 PM if following the suggested late-afternoon route.

July 14

Matterhorn sunrise, Glacier Paradise, and Riffelsee sunset

This is the full Zermatt highlight day: Kirchbrucke alpenglow, Matterhorn Glacier Paradise, Schwarzsee, then a second chance at Gornergrat and Riffelsee.

  • 05:30-06:30: Kirchbrucke for Matterhorn alpenglow.
  • 08:30-15:00: Matterhorn Glacier Paradise, Glacier Palace, observation platform, and Schwarzsee.
  • 16:00-19:30: Riffelsee reflections and golden-hour views if weather is good.
  1. 05:30
    Kirchbrucke sunrise

    Walk from town and watch the first warm light hit the Matterhorn.

  2. 08:30-15:00
    Matterhorn Glacier Paradise

    Go up to the high viewing platform, visit Glacier Palace, photograph 4000-meter peaks, then descend toward Schwarzsee.

  3. 16:00-19:30
    Gornergrat + Riffelsee

    Return to the Gornergrat Railway corridor for the best reflection and evening-light window.

July 15-16

Scenic rail to Zurich, then fly home

Use the Saver Day Pass for a scenic train day through the Mattertal valley, Andermatt, and Lucerne, then finish with Zurich Old Town, Lake Zurich, and the return flight.

  • Zermatt 07:06 -> Visp 08:19 -> Andermatt 10:20 -> Lucerne 13:41 -> Zurich HB 15:55.
  • Lucerne lockers are the most convenient luggage option during the city walk.
  • July 16 return: Zurich 11:20 -> Dublin 12:50; Dublin 15:25 -> Seattle 17:10.
  1. 07:06
    Leave Zermatt

    Best seat is left side leaving Zermatt for the Mattertal valley.

  2. 10:20-11:50
    Stop in Andermatt

    Explore the village, old church square, Reuss River, and Urseren Valley views.

  3. 13:41-15:09
    Lucerne walk

    Store bags at station lockers, then walk Kapellbrucke, Old Town streets, and Musegg Wall viewpoint.

  4. 18:00-22:00
    Zurich evening

    Walk Bahnhofstrasse, Lindenhof, St. Peter, Limmat River, Grossmunster, Fraumunster, lake promenade, and chocolate shops.
